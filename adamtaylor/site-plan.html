Adam the Person

I'm delighted to have you visit my website, and I hope your time here provides you with insights into my abilities as a Software Engineer while allowing you to get to know me better. My passion for learning has been a lifelong journey. From a young age, I demonstrated a knack for reading, mathematics, and science. I eagerly explored science museums, engaged in various competitions, and voraciously devoured every book within my reach. To this day, my love for learning endures, and each book I read fuels my curiosity, leaving me with even more questions and an insatiable thirst for knowledge. Running has been a cherished part of my life for as long as I can remember. It all began in Kindergarten when I engaged in a playful race with a local dog along the schoolyard fence. This early experience fostered both my physical strength and my deep affection for the sport. Over the years, I've participated in races spanning from 1-mile sprints to half marathons (13.1 miles), with ambitions to tackle a full marathon in the near future. Beyond my professional and athletic pursuits, I am a devoted family man. My beautiful wife and children hold a special place in my heart, and our shared love for outdoor adventures, such as hiking and camping, as well as our enthusiasm for exploring new culinary experiences, brings us closer together. We relish the time spent together as co-adventurers, cherishing these moments as a family.

The Scientist

In high school, my deep-rooted love for science became evident, and I channeled most of my energy into this subject. I delved into books encompassing Physics, Biology, Chemistry, and Geology, with a particular penchant for those related to animals. Memorizing scientific names became a quirky but enjoyable pastime. Years later, when I entered university, I pursued a degree in Human Biology, and my dedication paid off with outstanding results. I often found myself tutoring fellow students and engaging in enlightening conversations with my professors, delving into subjects that couldn't be fully explored in our standard curriculum. During my spare hours, I could be found in the microbiology lab, crafting agar plates and conducting small experiments whenever the professor was away. Graduating from Brigham Young University was a mixture of excitement and nostalgia, as I had achieved my academic goals, but it marked the end of a significant chapter in my life. Shortly after obtaining my degree, I secured a position at a laboratory close to my hometown, where I acquired the skills necessary to create and nurture embryos in small petri dishes, a technique known as In Vitro Fertilization. Since 2018, I have been continuously learning and growing within this company, cherishing both the work I do and the wonderful colleagues I share it with. I now oversee the lab's operations and collaborate with veterinarians to expand our client base and consistently enhance our results.

The Software Engineer

During my time at Brigham Young University, I dabbled in Computer Science classes out of sheer curiosity. To my delight, I discovered that I enjoyed them, and many of the concepts seemed to click with me. As I continued my journey working at the laboratory, I found myself increasingly drawn to coding and began taking small courses to develop my skills. Part of this newfound interest was sparked by my brother-in-law, a seasoned software engineer, whose descriptions of his work piqued my curiosity and motivated me to explore further. Subsequently, I made the decision to pursue a Bachelor's degree in Software Development at Brigham Young University, with the aim of delving deeper into the field and honing my skills. My ultimate aspiration is to embark on a career as a Software Engineer. This website serves not only as a portfolio but also as a representation of my progress. Over the years, it will showcase a growing collection of projects that I undertake. As I continue to learn and evolve, the site will serve as a testament to my ability to create and maintain websites, further demonstrating my expertise in the field.
